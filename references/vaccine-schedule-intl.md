# International / European Vaccine Schedule Reference

Source basis: WHO EPI (Expanded Programme on Immunization), European CDC (ECDC), 
UK NHS, German STIKO, and AAP (American Academy of Pediatrics) recommendations.

Use this file when user selects "international/European standards" during onboarding,
or when comparing a child's record to global best practices.

## China vs International: Key Differences

| Topic | China NIP | International Best Practice |
|-------|-----------|----------------------------|
| MMR Dose 1 timing | 8 months | 12 months (EU/US); earlier in China due to maternal antibody waning faster in higher-risk settings |
| BCG | Given at birth | Not given in low-TB countries (UK, Germany, etc.) |
| PCV | Not in NIP (self-paid) | Universally recommended and NIP-included in EU/US |
| Hib | Not in NIP (self-paid) | Universally recommended and NIP-included in EU/US |
| Rotavirus | Not in NIP (self-paid) | In NIP in UK, US, EU |
| IPV/bOPV mix | 2 IPV + 2 OPV | Full IPV in EU/US (no live OPV) |
| HepA | NIP in China (18m) | Not universal NIP in EU; recommended in US |

**Recommendation for families following international standards in China:**
- Treat PCV, Hib, Rotavirus as mandatory (not optional)
- Consider full IPV series rather than mixing with bOPV (available at paid clinics)
- Consider 12-month MMR (coordinate with clinic — requires a waiver from standard 8m schedule)
- Get annual flu vaccine — not well-emphasized in Chinese public health system

## European Country-Specific Notes

| Country | Distinctive recommendations |
|---------|----------------------------|
| 🇩🇪 Germany (STIKO) | MenC at 12m, MenACWY at 16-23m, BCG not given, full IPV |
| 🇬🇧 UK (NHS) | MenB at 8w/16w/12m (unique to UK), Rotavirus at 8w/12w, flu nasal spray from 2yr |
| 🇫🇷 France | Hexavalent vaccine (DTaP-IPV-Hib-HepB) as standard from 2m |
| 🇸🇪 Sweden | MenC not routine (low incidence); BCG only for high-risk |
| 🇺🇸 US (AAP/CDC) | HepA in NIP; full IPV; Rotavirus in NIP; HPV for boys |

When user's child splits time between China and Europe, default to the MORE comprehensive schedule (typically international), and note which vaccines may need to be obtained at international clinics in China.
